Dramatic Coast Guard Rescue: 4 People Saved from Yacht Off Long Island

By MICHAEL GRANT

April 6, 2026

This past weekend, emergency responders had to deal with a life-threatening incident in the waters of New York, which showed how unpredictable the Atlantic Ocean can be.In a very spectacular rescue by the Coast Guard, officials were pushed to their limits as they tried to get to a ship in trouble.The operation, which was focused on the four people who were rescued from a yacht off Long Island, is a clear reminder of how fast a vacation can turn into a fight for survival against the elements.The bravery of the first responders became the main point of a story that thankfully ended with no deaths, even though there were very high risks involved.

Facing the Rage of the Sea at Moriches Inlet

The problems started when the disabled boat "Proudfoot" lost power and had to deal with a weather system that was getting worse.The ship and its crew were suddenly faced with 12-foot swells that could have capsized the boat at any moment. They were near the dangerous Moriches Inlet.The U.S. Coast Guard had to be very careful and have nerves of steel to stop the drifting yacht this terrible weekend.The approach was almost impossible because of the strong gusts and huge waves, but the rescue swimmers and boat crews kept going through the worst of the storm to get to the people who were stuck.

The successful rescue of four sailors

When the U.S. Coast Guard got there, their top priority was the safety of the four sailors who were stuck on the listing ship.The evacuation process was exceedingly hard. The rescue personnel had to timing their maneuvers properly between the breaking waves to make sure everyone got to safety.After a tense time of planning and executing tactics, the expedition was pronounced a success, and all four people were taken back to shore for medical tests.The crew was unharmed, but the disabled yacht "Proudfoot" stayed afloat and eventually sank as it proceeded near the coast because of the strong currents.

The Proudfoot Grounds on Fire Island

This morning, the ship washed up on Fire Island, making the last episode of this maritime disaster public.Residents and local officials found the broken pieces of the yacht on the sand, which had been a quiet witness to the bad weather over the weekend.People are talking about salvage efforts to get rid of the debris and stop any more damage to the protected beaches.The yacht is completely lost, but the focus is still on the amazing teamwork and bravery that led to a victorious outcome for everyone involved in this difficult Long Island rescue.
