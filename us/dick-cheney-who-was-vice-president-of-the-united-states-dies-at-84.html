Dick Cheney, who was Vice President of the United States, dies at 84

By MICHAEL GRANT

November 4, 2025

Dick Cheney, who used to be Vice President, died at the age of 84. He was strong, but he often made things worse. His death ends one of the most important political careers in US history. People in the U.S. still talk about Cheney's decades of work in government, how he changed U.S. foreign policy, and how he led the country after 9/11.These are all very important issues when it comes to the president's power, security, and authority.

Early Life and Rising to the Top in Washington

Dick Cheney was born in Nebraska and grew up in Wyoming.He started his political career when he was young and eventually got elected to the U.S. House of Representatives.He quickly moved up the ranks in the government.Before he became vice president, he was the White House Chief of Staff, which made him a trusted political strategist.Cheney was later President George H. W. Bush's Secretary of Defense.He was in charge of the U.S. military during the Gulf War.These wins laid the groundwork for what would be his most important job.

Cheney's Job as Vice President

Cheney was the 46th Vice President from 2001 to 2009, when George W. Bush was in charge.The office had more power than it ever had before when he was in charge.Cheney was in charge of the country's national security strategy after the attacks on September 11.He was at the center of America's "war on terror" because he strongly supported the invasion of Iraq, the expansion of intelligence capabilities, and the use of harsher interrogation methods.Some people thought these things were great for the country's safety, while others thought they went too far.They made Cheney's position as one of the most powerful vice presidents in U.S. history even stronger.

An important but controversial legacy

There is still a lot of disagreement about what Cheney left behind.Some people thought he was a good leader who made tough choices when things got tough.Some people think that his policies, especially those about the Iraq War, had bad effects that lasted a long time.Cheney's political power is directly related to the debates about the president's spying, getting involved in other countries' business, and power.He kept defending what the government did even after he left office, and he was still a strong voice in talks about U.S. security and world leadership.

Their Political Views in Later Years

Cheney talked more about how the Republican Party was changing as he got older.He was always worried about where the party was going, especially when Donald Trump started to get more popular.When he publicly backed his daughter Liz Cheney, who is against Trumpism, it showed even more that he believes in traditional conservative values.His political power shaped the first few decades, but in his later years, the modern GOP showed how divided it was and how the effects of the Bush-Cheney era were fading.

Health Issues and Final Days

Cheney has had a lot of health problems over the years.He had a lot of heart attacks and got a new heart in 2012.He had heart problems for a long time, but he still took part in public life.People think he died because of problems that pneumonia and heart disease caused.

MICHAEL GRANT
