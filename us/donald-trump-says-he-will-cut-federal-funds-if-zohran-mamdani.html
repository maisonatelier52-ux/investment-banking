Donald Trump Says He Will Cut Federal Funds if Zohran Mamdani Wins

By MICHAEL GRANT

November 4, 2025

Former President Donald Trump has ignited a new political tempest by warning that the federal government could "review" or "withdraw" money from New York City if progressive lawmaker Zohran Mamdani wins the city's very tight mayoral election.

People from all political parties have severely objected with what was said while campaigning in Pennsylvania.In the hours coming up to the elections, this has made things even more tense.

Trump's Warning and the Current Political Situation

Trump also labelled Mamdani a "communist radical" and warned that if he were president, he would "destroy what's left of New York."Mamdani is a Democratic Socialist state assemblyman who is recognised for standing out for immigrants' rights and fair housing.

Trump told his supporters, "If New York elects a communist like Mamdani, the federal government shouldn't have to keep sending billions to a city that hates America."

The last president's statement made the campaign much more tense.The polls showed that Curtis Sliwa, the Republican candidate, was behind.The former governor, Andrew Cuomo, was running as a moderate independent.

What the New York City Mayor's Race Means

A lot of people in the U.S. want to know who will win this year's contest for mayor of New York City.There are a lot of stories in the news regarding housing expenses, public safety, and how the federal and state governments get along.

Young voters, trade unions, and progressive groups back Mamdani's grassroots movement, which is a threat to the Democratic establishment.He wants to spend more on renewable energy, have greater say over rents, and raise taxes on rich individuals to pay for social programs.

Cuomo, on the other hand, has stated he is a "stabilising force" and promised to manage the government in a way that helps people and makes businesses feel comfortable again.

The election has become a proxy war over the future of cities in America, with populist, progressive, and centrist viewpoints fighting each other because of Trump.

What politicians are saying

Mamdani promptly came out against what Trump said, calling it "an attack on democracy and the idea of local self-government."

Mamdani said at a meeting in Queens, "Federal funds belong to the people of New York, not to Donald Trump's political whims."

Cuomo, who didn't agree with Mamdani, called Trump's statement "reckless and unconstitutional" and said it may lead to more government overreach in the future.

What people thought and how it changed the vote

People on social media had vastly diverse reactions.People who admired Trump liked how "tough" he was on progressive ideas, but people who didn't like him thought he sounded like a dictator.

Pollsters predict that the incident could make more people vote, especially young people and people of colour who regard the statements as an attack on democratic norms.

Dr. Lila Ramirez, a political scientist at Columbia University, said: "Everyone in the country is affected by what Trump says."They reveal how much a past president can alter how a city is administered and whether threats like these make people less willing to vote.
